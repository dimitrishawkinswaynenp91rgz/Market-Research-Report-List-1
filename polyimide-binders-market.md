<p><p>Polyimide binders are used in various industries for their high-temperature resistance, chemical stability, and mechanical strength. These binders are classified into three main types based on their chemical structure: condensation polymers, addition polymers, and hybrid polymers. Condensation polymers are formed through the condensation reaction between two or more monomers, while addition polymers are formed through addition reactions. Hybrid polymers combine the characteristics of both condensation and addition polymers, offering a unique combination of properties for specific applications in the market.</p></p>
