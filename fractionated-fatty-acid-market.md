<p><p>Fractionated Fatty Acids are divided into three main types based on their chain length: VFA (Very Long Chain Fatty Acids), MCFA (Medium Chain Fatty Acids), and LCFA (Long Chain Fatty Acids). VFA typically have more than 18 carbon atoms and are commonly used in industries such as cosmetics. MCFA have 6-12 carbon atoms and are used in various health and food products. LCFA have 12-22 carbon atoms and are widely utilized in the production of soaps and detergents.</p></p>
