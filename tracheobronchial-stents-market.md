<p><p>Tracheobronchial stents are medical devices designed to treat obstructive diseases in the airway, such as cancer, strictures, or tracheobronchomalacia. These stents are tubular in shape and are usually made of metallic materials, such as stainless steel or nitinol, or biocompatible polymers. They are inserted into the airway to maintain its patency, allowing the passage of air and facilitating normal breathing.</p><p>The tracheobronchial stents market has experienced significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 12.4% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, an increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, including lung cancer, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and tracheal stenosis, is driving the demand for tracheobronchial stents. Secondly, advancements in stent technology, such as the development of self-expandable stents and the use of biodegradable materials, have improved the effectiveness and safety of these devices.</p><p>Additionally, the rising geriatric population, who are more susceptible to respiratory diseases, is also contributing to the market growth. Moreover, the growing adoption of minimally invasive procedures, which often involve the use of tracheobronchial stents, is further fueling the market expansion. Minimally invasive procedures offer benefits such as shorter hospital stays, reduced recovery time, and lower morbidity rates.</p><p>However, the market also faces challenges, such as the high cost of tracheobronchial stents and the risk of complications associated with their use.
