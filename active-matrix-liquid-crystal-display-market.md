<p><p>Active-matrix Liquid-crystal Display (AMOLED) panels are commonly categorized into two main types based on the material used for the thin-film transistors: Polycrystalline silicon (or polysilicon) and Amorphous silicon. Polysilicon panels offer higher resolution and faster pixel response times, making them ideal for high-end applications such as smartphones and TVs. On the other hand, amorphous silicon panels are more cost-effective and energy-efficient, making them suitable for budget-friendly devices like entry-level smartphones and monitors. Both types have their own advantages and are widely used in the display industry.</p></p>
