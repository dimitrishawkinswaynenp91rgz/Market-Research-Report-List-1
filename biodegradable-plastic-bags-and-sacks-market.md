<p><p>Biodegradable plastic bags and sacks are classified into different types based on the materials used for their production. Polylactic acid (PLA) is derived from renewable resources like corn starch and is compostable. Polyhydroxyalkanoate (PHA) is a biopolymer produced by microorganisms. Starch blends are a combination of natural starch and other biodegradable polymers. Other types include materials like polycaprolactone (PCL) and polybutylene succinate (PBS). These biodegradable options are eco-friendly alternatives to traditional plastic bags, reducing pollution and environmental impact.</p></p>
