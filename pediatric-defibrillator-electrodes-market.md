<p><p>Pediatric defibrillator electrodes are used to deliver electrical shocks to restore normal heart rhythm in children experiencing cardiac arrest. There are two types of pediatric defibrillator electrodes: internal and external. Internal electrodes are implanted directly on or inside the heart during surgery. External electrodes are placed on the child's chest and connected to a defibrillator machine. Both types of electrodes play a crucial role in saving lives by quickly and effectively treating cardiac emergencies in pediatric patients.</p></p>
