<p><p>The Lithium-Metal Secondary Battery Market consists of two main types: Li/Intercalant Cathode and Li/Sulfur. In Li/Intercalant Cathode batteries, lithium ions move between the anode and cathode through an intercalation compound, such as lithium cobalt oxide. On the other hand, Li/Sulfur batteries utilize a sulfur cathode that undergoes a series of chemical reactions with lithium ions during charge and discharge cycles. Both types offer high energy density and potential for improved performance in various applications.</p></p>
