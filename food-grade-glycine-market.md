<p><p>Food Grade Glycine market is segmented into three types: Strecker, Hydantion, and Biosynthesis. In Strecker method, glycine is produced from ammonium sulfate and formaldehyde. Hydantion method involves the reaction of ammonia and cyanogen chloride. Biosynthesis is a natural process where living organisms produce glycine from serine. Each method has its own production process and applications in the food industry. These different types cater to various needs of manufacturers and consumers looking for high-quality food grade glycine.</p></p>
