<p><p>Plasma Enhanced Chemical Vapor Deposition (PECVD) Systems are categorized into two main types: Anode PECVD Systems and Cathode PECVD Systems. Anode PECVD Systems involve the use of positively charged anodes to attract negatively charged ions, resulting in a controlled deposition process. On the other hand, Cathode PECVD Systems utilize negatively charged cathodes to attract positively charged ions for deposition. Both types of systems play a crucial role in various industries such as semiconductor manufacturing, optical coatings, and nanotechnology.</p></p>
