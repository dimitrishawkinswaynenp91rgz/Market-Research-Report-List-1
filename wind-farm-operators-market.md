<p><p>Wind farm operators can be classified into Type I and Type II markets based on their market types. In Type I markets, operators have fixed tariffs or power purchase agreements with utility companies, providing stable and predictable revenue streams. In contrast, Type II markets involve operators selling electricity directly into the wholesale market, where prices fluctuate based on supply and demand. Type I operators have lower revenue risks but may miss out on potential upside, while Type II operators have higher revenue variability but also greater potential for profits.</p></p>
