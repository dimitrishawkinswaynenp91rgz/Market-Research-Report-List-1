Self-Supported rotary steam joints are designed to be self-contained and do not rely on any external support structures. On the other hand, Bracket/Rod-Supported rotary steam joints require additional brackets or rods to hold them in place. Both types have their advantages and are chosen based on factors such as application requirements, space limitations, and ease of installation.</p></p>
