<p><p>Scintillators are materials that emit light when they interact with ionizing radiation. There are three main types of scintillator markets: solid, liquid, and gaseous. Solid scintillators, such as crystals or plastics, are commonly used in radiation detection equipment. Liquid scintillators are often used in applications requiring high sensitivity, such as nuclear power plants or particle physics experiments. Gaseous scintillators, like xenon gas, are used in some medical imaging devices and other specialized applications. Each type of scintillator has unique properties that make it suitable for different purposes.</p></p>
