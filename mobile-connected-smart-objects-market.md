<p><p>Direct Connectivity in the Mobile Connected Smart Objects market refers to devices that can connect directly to a mobile network without the need for an intermediary device. These objects have built-in cellular capabilities and can connect to the internet directly. Indirect Connectivity, on the other hand, involves smart objects that require an intermediary or hub device to connect to the mobile network. These hub devices act as a bridge between the smart objects and the mobile network, enabling communication and control. Both types play a crucial role in the Internet of Things (IoT) ecosystem.</p></p>
