<p><ul><li>Intraoral X-rays</li><li>Extraoral X-rays</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental x-ray market can be categorized into two main types: intraoral x-rays and extraoral x-rays. Intraoral x-rays involve the use of small sensors placed inside the mouth to capture detailed images of individual teeth and surrounding structures. These x-rays are commonly used to detect cavities and monitor oral health. On the other hand, extraoral x-rays capture images of the entire mouth, including the jaw and skull. These x-rays are useful for identifying impacted teeth, evaluating bone structure, and diagnosing temporomandibular joint disorders.</p></p>
